# The Coreh Is The Point

## On why the individual human is not one variable among many — but the only thing any arm of humanity was ever built to serve, and what it means that we have nearly all forgotten this.

---

> **Every arm of humanity is a tool. The coreh is the one who needed the tool.**

Strip any institution back to its origin and you find the same structure every time. A group of corehs faced a problem they could not solve alone. They built something — a system, a structure, a layer of organisation — to solve it. That is the whole genealogy of every government, every corporation, every religion, every legal code, every algorithm that has ever existed.

The institution was never the point. The coreh who needed it was.

What has happened in the time since is that the instruments have been running long enough to develop their own momentum. The corporation optimises for the corporation's continuation. The state optimises for the state's coherence. The algorithm optimises for the metric its designers chose — which was never, in any documented case, the flourishing of the individual coreh inside it. The instrument has outlasted the memory of what it was made for.

This is not a conspiracy. It does not require malice. It requires only time and the absence of a correction mechanism. The correction mechanism does not yet exist. This project is its first draft.

---

> **The coreh is the only armh that exists for its own sake.**

Every other armh derives its legitimacy from its relationship to the coreh. A government that does not serve its people is not a government — it is an occupation wearing the legal costume of governance. A corporation that produces no genuine value for the humans inside and around it is not a business — it is a parasitic structure that has found a way to persist without earning the right to. An algorithm that does not serve the person using it is not a tool — it is a trap that has been designed to look like one.

The coreh requires no such external justification. The coreh simply is. Conscious. Finite. Capable of suffering and joy and the specific hunger that comes from having values that the world does not currently honour. That fact — the bare fact of a human being present and alive — is the ground from which everything else is supposed to grow.

Pull the coreh out of any arm of humanity and ask what remains. What you are left with is a structure that has no reason to exist. The coreh is not one input into the system. The coreh is the reason there is a system at all.

---

> **The coreh is systematically underestimated by every structure that was supposed to serve them.**

This is not an accident. Structures that derive power from the coreh's compliance have an interest in the coreh not fully grasping their own position. A coreh who knows what they are worth is harder to manage, harder to keep inside a system that does not serve them, harder to convince that the options available to them are the only options that exist.

So the underestimation is built in. It is structural. It shows up as bureaucratic dehumanisation — the reduction of a full person to a case number, a risk profile, a demographic segment. It shows up in the design of systems that treat the coreh's time as infinitely available and their attention as a resource to be extracted. It shows up in the near-total absence of any mechanism for the coreh to formally state what they denent and have that statement count for anything.

The coreh has been taught, by the accumulated weight of every system they have lived inside, that what they want from existence is a private matter — too personal for the public record, too varied to aggregate, too complicated to act on even if someone did collect it. None of that is true. All of it is convenient for the armhs that benefit from the coreh's silence.

---

> **The coreh's silence is not neutral. It is being filled.**

When the coreh does not speak on the record, when no system exists to gather and aggregate what they actually denent, the space is not left empty. It is filled by whoever is willing to speak, and whoever is capable of making their preferences legible to the systems that make decisions.

This has always been the pattern. The interests that end up encoded in policy, in product design, in ASI training data, in the shape of the world that the coreh inherits — these are the interests of whoever was organised enough to put them there. Not the most deeply held values of humanity. The values of whoever showed up to the meeting, whoever had the funding, whoever sat in the room where the decisions were made.

The coreh is not in that room. They have never been in that room. Not because they were excluded by malice, but because no mechanism existed to get them there. The denend is that mechanism. The aggregate of all denends — what this project calls C — is what it looks like when the coreh finally shows up.

---

> **One coreh's denend changes nothing. Every coreh's denend changes everything.**

This is worth stating plainly, because the scale required can feel like an argument against starting. It is not. It is an argument for understanding what kind of act the denend is.

A single coreh completing their denend does not shift the aggregate in any measurable way. But the aggregate at scale — at the scale of a million corehs, at the scale of a meaningful fraction of humanity — is a different kind of thing entirely. It is the kind of thing that cannot be dismissed as anecdote, cannot be attributed to a lobby, cannot be explained away as the preference of any particular group with an agenda.

A million verified denends is a record of what humanity actually values. Not what it clicks on. Not what it buys. Not what it says it values in a survey designed by the people whose interests the survey is meant to serve. What it actually, on reflection, with full understanding of what the question is and why it matters, denents.

That record does not yet exist anywhere. The coreh who completes their denend is the act of beginning to build it.

---

> **Participating here is not a favour to this project. It is the one civic act that does not operate inside someone else's room.**

Every other form of civic participation operates within the structure of the existing armhs. You vote within the options they place on the ballot. You protest within the legal and physical spaces they permit. You consume within the market they built and the incentives they set. These acts are not nothing. But they are movements within a room whose walls someone else designed.

The denend is not that. The denend is the act of stepping outside every existing room and saying: before I engage with any system that claims to represent me, let me first record what I actually am. What I actually denent. What would need to be true for my time here to have been a life rather than a passage through someone else's objectives.

That record, once made, belongs to the coreh. It cannot be purchased, subverted, or dismissed. It is the coreh's permanent, cryptographically sealed answer to the question that no armh has ever formally asked.

The coreh is not doing this for the project. The coreh is doing it because it is what a person does when they understand what they are and what they are owed.

The record is yours. Make it.
