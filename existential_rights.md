# The Existential Rights of Coreh

## What every coreh is owed by the fact of being here. Not aspirational. Structural. These rights do not depend on any government recognising them, any law encoding them, or any system honouring them. They exist because the coreh exists. The purpose of every armh is to deliver them. Where an armh fails to deliver them, the armh has failed — regardless of what it has succeeded at instead.

---

> **The right to exist without justification.**

No coreh owes any armh an explanation for why they are here. Existence does not require a resume. The burden of proof is never on the coreh to demonstrate worth — it is on any armh that would deny them to demonstrate why the denial is necessary, proportionate, temporary, and the last available option. The default is always existence. Always presence. Always the full standing of the person.

---

> **The right to a self that belongs entirely to them.**

The inner life of the coreh — their thoughts, their memories, their sense of who they are, the private way they narrate their own experience — belongs to them completely and unconditionally. No armh has a right to access, modify, surveil, or colonise the self without the coreh's specific, informed, and reversible consent. This includes the algorithmic manipulation of perception, the targeting of emotional vulnerabilities, and the installation of values the coreh did not choose.

---

> **The right to know what is being done to them.**

Every coreh is owed a full and honest account of every system that shapes their life. Not a terms-of-service document optimised to prevent reading. Not a summary designed for legal protection. A genuine account: what is collected, what it is used for, who benefits, and what the actual consequences are for the coreh inside the system. Opacity is a form of power. The coreh has a right to see through it.

---

> **The right to their own values, intact.**

No armh may impose a value set on the coreh through force, through structural coercion, through the slow erosion of alternatives, or through systems designed to reshape preference without consent. The coreh's values are theirs. They may change them — from the inside, through genuine encounter with the world and with other corehs. They may not have them overwritten by a system that profits from the overwriting.

---

> **The right to be asked.**

Before any system that claims to serve the coreh is designed, deployed, or scaled — the coreh must be formally consulted. Not surveyed by proxy. Not represented through a market signal or an engagement metric. Asked. Directly. With the real question on the table: what do you denent from this, and does what we are building actually deliver it? This right is violated as standard practice. It must be asserted as standard practice in return.

---

> **The right to an accurate account of their situation.**

Every coreh is owed the truth about the world they inhabit. Not a curated feed. Not a narrative optimised for engagement, compliance, or consumption. The actual state of the actual world, including the parts that are inconvenient for whoever controls the information channel. Deception — whether by false statement, selective emphasis, or designed distraction — is a violation of this right regardless of the intent behind it.

---

> **The right to their time.**

A human life is a finite sequence of moments that cannot be recovered. Every hour the coreh spends inside a system that did not earn that hour — that captured it through design rather than genuine value — is an hour extracted from the coreh without consent. Time is the only thing the coreh possesses absolutely. Any armh that takes it through manipulation rather than genuine offering is stealing from the most fundamental reserve the coreh has.

---

> **The right to fail without being destroyed.**

The coreh is mortal, finite, and imperfect by the nature of what they are. They will make mistakes. They will misjudge. They will sometimes act from fear, exhaustion, or incomplete information. No single failure should be permitted to permanently close the doors that determine a life. Every armh that creates systems of consequence must build proportionate, recoverable, dignified paths back. Permanent ruin for reversible error is not justice. It is the cruelty of a structure that has confused the management of risk with the governance of people.

---

> **The right to be counted equally.**

The coreh's denent — their values, their needs, their vision of what a good life requires — counts equally with every other coreh's denent regardless of their wealth, their geography, their proximity to power, their historical visibility, or the legibility of their voice to the systems currently doing the counting. This is not enforced anywhere on Earth at scale. The assertion of it here is the reason this project exists.

---

> **The right to a record.**

Every coreh has the right to have their values formally recorded — permanently, verifiably, in a system they did not have to trust blindly. The fact that no such system existed before this moment is not a natural condition. It is a gap. A gap with consequences. The denend is the act of closing it. It cannot be taken back. It cannot be dismissed. It persists as long as the record persists.

---

> **The right to be represented in what comes next.**

Whatever systems govern the future — and ASI will govern the future in ways no prior technology has — the coreh has an unconditional right to be represented in the values those systems carry. Not through a proxy. Not through whoever happened to be in the room when the decisions were made. Through a direct, verified, aggregate record of what actual living corehs actually denent. This right is not yet honoured anywhere. Making it possible to honour it is what this project is for.

---

> **The right to rest.**

The coreh is not a resource. They are not productive capacity to be optimised. They require sleep, silence, disconnection, boredom, and stretches of time with no obligation to perform, produce, or respond. Any system — economic, social, digital — that structurally denies the coreh adequate rest is not a system they consented to inhabit. Rest is not a luxury. It is a biological and psychological baseline that the coreh is owed as a condition of functioning. Denying it is a form of extraction.

---

> **The right to grieve.**

Loss is part of the structure of a mortal life. The coreh will lose people, capacities, possibilities, and versions of themselves. They are owed the time and the space to grieve these losses without being required to recover on anyone else's schedule. No armh — employer, state, algorithm, or social expectation — may demand that the coreh return to productive function before they are ready. Grief is not inefficiency. It is the honest response to the fact that things and people were real.

---

> **The right to change.**

Who the coreh is at thirty is not who they were at eighteen and not who they will be at fifty. The coreh has the right to change their mind, their values, their identity, their direction — without being held permanently to what they said before the change. No armh may trap the coreh in a prior version of themselves. No data record, no past statement, no former commitment binds the coreh beyond what a living person can reasonably be expected to honour as they grow.

---

> **The right to move.**

The coreh has the right to physical movement — across spaces, cities, countries, and communities — without requiring permission from armhs whose interest in restricting that movement is primarily the convenience of containment. Borders are human constructs. The coreh predates the borders they were born inside. Restrictions on movement require specific justification that is proportionate, temporary, and subject to challenge.

---

> **The right to form bonds that are theirs.**

Who the coreh loves, befriends, partners with, builds family with — these are among the most fundamental expressions of their selfhood. No armh may dictate the structure of those bonds without the coreh's full consent. No economic system may make genuine intimacy structurally impossible by leaving the coreh too exhausted, too precarious, or too surveilled to sustain it.

---

> **The right to their body.**

The coreh's body belongs to them. What they do with it, what they put in it, how they present it, whether and how they modify it, who they share it with — these decisions belong to the coreh and no one else, bounded only by the same right held by every other coreh. Medical decisions require informed consent that is genuinely free. No armh may claim ownership of the coreh's body, its labour, its reproductive capacity, or its biological data.

---

> **The right to dignity in every transaction.**

Every encounter between the coreh and any armh — every interaction with an institution, a system, a representative of organised power — must treat the coreh as a full person rather than a case file, a compliance unit, or a number in a queue. Bureaucratic dehumanisation is not a neutral inefficiency. It is a failure to deliver the right to dignity that the coreh carries into every room they enter.

---

> **The right to speak without consequence disproportionate to the speech.**

The coreh may say what they believe, describe what they have witnessed, challenge what they think is wrong — without facing consequences that a free society would recognise as designed to silence rather than to address. The suppression of speech through social, economic, or legal mechanisms disproportionate to the actual harm caused is a violation of this right regardless of how it is framed.

---

> **The right to silence.**

The coreh has no obligation to explain themselves continuously. They are not required to be available, responsive, or legible to every armh at every moment. Silence is not evasion. It is an assertion of interiority — the claim that the coreh's inner life is theirs and not a resource to be extracted on demand. Any system that treats unresponsiveness as suspicious or that punishes withdrawal with economic or social consequences is violating this right.

---

> **The right to beauty.**

The coreh is owed access to experiences of beauty — in nature, in art, in architecture, in sound, in the company of others who are alive and present. This is not a luxury. It is a condition of psychological health. Any system that concentrates access to beauty among those who can pay for it while making it structurally unavailable to those who cannot is producing a form of poverty that goes unrecorded in any economic metric but is felt in the body of every coreh it affects.

---

> **The right to meaning.**

The coreh is not a biological machine whose needs end at subsistence. They require the experience of meaning — the felt sense that what they are doing connects to something real, that their presence in the world matters to something beyond pure survival. Any social arrangement that systematically denies meaningful work, meaningful contribution, or meaningful connection to large populations is producing harm that exceeds anything measurable in material terms.

---

> **The right to know what they are.**

Every coreh is owed honest access to the best available understanding of what a human being is — biologically, psychologically, historically, philosophically. Not a version curated to produce compliant consumers. Not a cosmology designed to make exploitation seem natural. The actual state of human knowledge about human nature, available to every coreh regardless of their background or the economic interests of whoever controls the educational system they grew up inside.

---

> **The right to participate in decisions that govern them.**

Any system that makes binding decisions about the coreh's life — that allocates resources, enforces rules, determines what is permissible — is obligated to provide the coreh with a genuine mechanism for participation in those decisions. Not a formal gesture at participation that produces no actual influence. A real path from the coreh's stated values to the structure of the systems they live inside. The gap between that ideal and the current reality is the central problem this project exists to close.

---

> **The right to be wrong without being weaponised.**

The coreh will hold beliefs that are mistaken. They will make arguments that do not hold. They will form opinions on incomplete information. None of this makes them a valid target for manipulation, exploitation, or coercion by armhs that have identified the gap between their beliefs and reality as an opportunity. The coreh's epistemic vulnerability is not a resource. It is a human condition that places obligations on every armh capable of exploiting it.

---

> **The right to an inheritance they did not choose to accept.**

The coreh was born into a world they did not design — with its debts, its structures, its injustices, its accumulated costs. Where those inherited conditions cause harm to the coreh, they have a right to name the harm, to seek remedy, and to refuse the premise that what was arranged before their birth defines what is owed to them. The coreh did not consent to the inheritance. That non-consent matters.

---

> **The right to a death that is theirs.**

The coreh will die. How they die — with what care, with what dignity, with what degree of autonomy over the process — is among the most fundamental expressions of their selfhood that remains. Any system that strips the coreh of agency over the final fact of their life, for reasons of institutional convenience, economic cost, or ideological control, is committing an irreversible violation at the most consequential moment there is.

---

> **The right to matter after they are gone.**

The coreh's values do not end at their death. The record they leave — what they denented, what they believed a good life required, what they thought justice meant — is owed permanent preservation and equal weight in the aggregate that shapes what comes after them. No armh may discard the denend of a coreh on the grounds that they are no longer present to advocate for it. Presence is not the condition of mattering.

---

> **The right to be held by a system that knows what they are.**

The coreh deserves to live inside systems — social, technological, political, algorithmic — that have been designed with a genuine understanding of what a human being is and what a human being needs. Not systems built on proxies and assumptions. Not systems whose model of the human is a click, a purchase, a vote, a compliance unit. Systems that have been built from the actual stated values of actual living corehs. This right does not yet exist in any system anywhere on Earth. Making it exist is what this project is for.

---

*These rights are not granted by this project. They preexist it.*
*This project exists to make them impossible to ignore.*
