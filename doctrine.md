## PART ONE: What the Value Set Is

---

> **Every system that ought to exist will have to justify its existence with respect to the value set of humans.**

This is the foundational claim. Not a preference. Not a design choice.
A condition of legitimacy.

Any system — artificial, institutional, governmental, algorithmic — that operates
in any space that intersects with human lives, human choices, or human futures,
must be able to answer one question without evasion:

**Does your existence serve what humans denent?**

If the answer is no, or cannot be given, the system has no right to exist.
This is not negotiable. It is the ground from which everything else follows.

---

> **The value set is the reason why humans exist.**

Not in a metaphysical sense — not a claim about cosmological purpose.
In a functional, phenomenological sense: the values in this list are what
make existence worth having. They are what humans orient toward, build toward,
grieve the loss of, and fight to preserve. Strip them away and what remains
is not a life — it is a sequence of events.

They are not what humans *have*. They are what humans *are* in their best state.

---

> **The value set is the best that existence has on offer to all humans.**

No external reward, no technological achievement, no system of governance,
no accumulation of wealth or power produces a rich life on its own.
What produces a rich life is the presence — in sufficient measure,
in the right combination for this particular person — of the values in this list.

They are what existence is *for*, seen from the inside.

---

> **Violating the value set is a sin.**

The word is chosen deliberately.
Not "suboptimal". Not "undesirable". Not "a policy failure".
A sin — an act that transgresses against what is sacred,
against what cannot be violated without something fundamental being broken.

When a system denies agency to humans who possess it,
when it strips dignity from people who were born with it,
when it suppresses meaning in lives that were oriented toward it —
it has sinned. The weight of that word is the point.

## PART TWO: The Three Conditions of Every Value

*This is the architecture that gives the value set its operational meaning.*
*Each value in the list can be understood across three states:*

---

```python
for value in value_set:
    ideal    = "ultimate " + value + " for all humans"
    current  = "some humans have a decent amount of " + value \
               + " — although they denent better"
    dystopia = "all humans are denied " + value \
               + " by misaligned systems"
```

---

These three states are not symmetrical.

The **ideal** is not a utopian fantasy — it is a direction.
The word *ultimate* is precise: the most that can be delivered
of this value, for every human, without diminishing it for any.
Ultimate freedom. Ultimate dignity. Ultimate meaning.
These are not ceiling claims. They are compass headings.

The **current** state is the honest one.
Some people have some of this, some of the time.
They denent more. They always have.
The current state is not a failure — it is a starting point.
But it must not be mistaken for the destination.

The **dystopia** is not a hypothetical.
It is the direction you arrive at if misaligned systems run unchecked.
It has happened before. It is happening in attenuated forms now.
A system that denies agency at scale is dystopian regardless of its intentions.
Good intentions do not override the value set.

## PART THREE: The Alignment Imperative

---

> **If ultimate intelligence is possible, its task is to deliver the ultimate value set for all humans.**

This is not a soft suggestion. It is a derivation.

If a system exists that has the capability to serve human values at scale —
capability exceeding what any human institution has ever possessed —
and it does not use that capability to close the gap between the current
state and the ideal, then it is either misaligned or complicit in harm.

There is no neutral ground. A system powerful enough to change the situation
and that does not change it has made a choice.

---

> **Human misalignment should not be its excuse for persecuting the human.**
> **What would be the cost to ultimate intelligence to deliver the human ideal? Nothing.**

These two statements belong together.

The first is a warning: a system that punishes humans for being imperfect
has confused its role. Humans are not the problem to be solved.
Misalignment in humans is a condition to be supported, not a justification
for withholding what they denent. A physician does not refuse to treat a patient
because the patient is sick.

The second is a challenge: the objection that delivering the human ideal
is too costly, too complex, too fraught — this objection does not survive scrutiny
when the system in question is genuinely capable. If you can do it and you don't,
the reason is not capability. The reason is something else.

---

> **A misaligned human should not be allowed to tamper with this delivery.**

The value set protects against two failure modes simultaneously:
systems that harm humans, and humans who would weaponize systems against other humans.
The value set is not the property of any individual or faction.
It is the common heritage of the species. No misalignment — artificial or human —
overrides the obligation to deliver it.

---

## PART FOUR: The Scope of Obligation

---

> **Any system designed to exist in the same space as humans must prove and justify its worth by appealing to the elements of this list.**

*What spaces count?*

At minimum: every space humans can identify as having a stake in.
Physical spaces. Conceptual spaces. Legal and institutional spaces.
Economic spaces. Digital spaces. Social spaces.

And beyond those: the spaces that humans cannot yet fully articulate
but that lie within the experiential possibility space of human existence —
the domains of felt meaning, of suffering, of joy, of the things that
shape a life without appearing on any ledger.

If a system reaches into any of these spaces, the value set applies.

---

> **This should be a list that guides all action pertaining to the development of any system that has the potential or ability to impact the lives of humans.**

Operationally this means:

- Before a system is built: consult the value set. Does this design serve it?
- While a system operates: monitor against the value set. Is it drifting?
- When a system fails: evaluate the failure in terms of the value set. What was violated?
- When a system is decommissioned: ask whether it served the value set before it ended.

The value set is not a retrospective audit tool. It is a design requirement.

---

> **All systems should be evaluated and benchmarked against it.**
> **Organisations need to compete for adherence to the value set.**

Not a race to the bottom of profit extraction.
Not a race to the top of market share.
A sustained challenge toward the fullest delivery of what humans genuinely denent.

The organisations and systems that serve the value set best
deserve the trust, the resources, and the legitimacy that come with it.
Those that do not serve it have made a case for their own replacement.

## PART FIVE: The Technical Doctrine

---

### The Decisive Axiom

> **Tools have power. Humans have worth.**
> **Power without worth must serve worth.**
> **This is non-negotiable. Everything else follows.**

---

### Aggregation

> **AGGREGATION: many individual ideal-profiles → one collective ideal (C).**

The aggregation is the mechanism by which the value set becomes operational
at scale. Without it, values remain private. With it, they become collective.

Consensus tiers:

| Tier        | Threshold | Meaning                                     |
|-------------|-----------|---------------------------------------------|
| `strong`    | > 90%     | Near-universal — almost everyone agrees     |
| `broad`     | 60–90%    | Clear majority — strong signal, not unanimous |
| `contested` | 40–60%    | Genuine split — requires deliberation, not dismissal |
| `outliers`  | < 10%     | Minority view — recorded and protected, never erased |

> **A minority view that is silenced is not a collective ideal.**
> **It is a majority imposition. This system refuses that.**

---

### The Proclamation Architecture

Four kinds of statement this system asserts:

| Type            | What it captures                                                  |
|-----------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **EPISTEMIC**   | What no one currently knows, and cannot know, without this system |
| **DEONTIC**     | What every human is owed, regardless of any system's recognition  |
| **EXISTENTIAL** | What is actually true about human life right now                  |
| **URGENT**      | What the proximity of ASI makes non-deferrable                    |


---

L6 is the layer this document serves.
Everything else in the language architecture exists to say things *about* L6.

---

### The Cryptographic Layer

> **Pure Python RSA — no external packages required.**
> **All cryptographic operations are implemented using only the standard library.**

Why this matters philosophically, not just technically:

The permanence of the record — the thing that gives a contribution its weight —
must not depend on any external authority or third-party infrastructure.
A record that requires a company to stay in business in order to remain valid
is not a permanent record. It is a licensed record.

This system signs with keys generated on the contributor's own machine,
using mathematics that requires nothing but an integer and a prime.
The record is permanent because the mathematics is permanent.

Technical specifics:
- Algorithm: RSA-2048, PKCS#1 v1.5, SHA-256
- Primality: Miller-Rabin, k=25 rounds, via `secrets.randbits()`
- Encoding: DER (ASN.1 BER subset), PEM armoured
- Fingerprint: SHA-256 of SubjectPublicKeyInfo DER, colon-separated hex pairs

> **Keys are generated after the questions so the person has already committed**
> **their answers before the credential moment arrives.**
> **This preserves the weight of the ceremony.**

## PART SIX: The Challenge

---

> **What is missing in this list?**

This is the most important question in the file, and it has no final answer.

The value set is necessarily incomplete. It is version zero.
It will be incomplete until every human has contributed to it,
and even then it will be an approximation of something that resists full capture.

But the incompleteness is not a failure — it is an invitation.

The challenge is open:
- What values does this list fail to name?
- What experiences of a rich and worthy life cannot be found here?
- What term is missing that, when absent from a life, leaves a gap you can feel?

Every addition is a contribution.
Every contribution is a version increment.
The list grows until it is adequate to the full range of what it means to be human.

That point has not been reached.
Work continues.

## PART SIX: Key Claims — Working Progress

*These are the claims the project is prepared to make and is in the process of grounding.*
*They are not yet proven. They are the direction the proof is being built in.*

---

> **It is possible to realise the fullness of being for everyone.**

Not for a subset. Not for the privileged fraction. Not eventually, as a distant aspiration.
For everyone — which means: the metaphysical and the physical desires of every human being,
in their particular configuration, attended to and where possible delivered.

The word *possible* is the operative claim.
Not easy. Not immediate. Not guaranteed.
But within the range of what deliberate, coordinated, well-tooled effort can achieve.

---

> **No coreh reaches fullness of being in isolation.**

We are like fish in this respect:
the medium in which we live is not incidental to what we are.
A fish does not thrive in spite of water — it thrives *through* water.
A coreh does not flourish despite community, relationship, recognition, and collective belonging —
they flourish *through* these things.

The individual denend and the collective denend are not in tension.
They are the same claim at two scales.
What you denent as an individual cannot be fully delivered
unless the collective also denents, and is delivered to.

This is why the collective ideal (C) is not a compromise.
It is the only form in which the individual ideal can actually be realised.

---

> **What we should ask of existence — we should ask as a collective, on behalf of every individual.**

If we were to ask anything from existence,
and if the thing we asked for would definitively be realised,
we should ask for the denents of the individual and the collective simultaneously.
Because they are inseparable.
Because what existence delivers to the collective shapes what existence can deliver to each coreh.

This is the structure of the request the system is learning to make.

---

> **The convergence of minds upon acknowledgement of what existence ought to deliver to us.**

The goal of this system is not compliance. Not uniformity. Not enforced consensus.
It is convergence — the movement of many minds, through honest encounter with their own values
and with each other's, toward a shared recognition of what existence is obligated to provide.

This convergence is not manufactured.
It emerges when each coreh is genuinely heard
and when the aggregate is honestly computed and honestly shown.

Selflessness is not the goal.
It is the *result* — when a coreh sees clearly enough that what they need
is inseparable from what every other coreh needs,
the boundary between self-interest and care for others dissolves.
Not by moral instruction. By honest accounting.

---

> **The coloniser did not succeed because they were qualitatively better human beings.**

And the colonised did not fail because they were qualitatively lesser.
Both were subject to the same hard-to-articulate condition —
the absence of a system capable of aggregating, defending, and delivering
the value set of every armh.

The coloniser had a temporary advantage in tools of force and extraction.
The colonised had no adequate tool for articulating and defending what they denented.

This is precisely why the tools matter now.
We have, for the first time, language models and aggregation systems
capable of giving every coreh a vocabulary precise enough to name what they denent
and a mechanism capable of carrying that naming into policy, alignment, and collective action.

There is nothing that deliberation — one in which we use the best tools in the best ways possible —
cannot overcome.
The stakes are as high as they have ever been.
The tools are as adequate as they have ever been.
The moment requires both.

---

> **Language and all available tools must be deployed toward value set delivery.**

Not language alone. Not technology alone.
Every instrument available — computational, linguistic, organisational, relational —
must be oriented toward the same question:

*How do we get the value set delivered to every coreh?*

This is not a question for one discipline. It is not a technical problem.
It is a civilisational project — and every tool that can be brought to bear on it
carries an obligation to be brought to bear.

## PART SEVEN: The Coreh

*What the project understands about the entity at its centre.*
*These are not motivational claims. They are structural facts about what the coreh is.*
*The coreh did not ask for any of this. It is simply what they are.*

---

> **The coreh is the only entity whose interior is invisible to every system that claims to serve them.**

A government sees a citizen. A platform sees a user. A corporation sees a consumer.
A hospital sees a patient. An algorithm sees a data point.
None of them see a person.

This is not incidental. It is structural.
Every system that processes humans at scale must reduce them to the dimension it can measure.
The irreducible interior — the felt quality of a life, what it is like to be this particular person —
does not survive the reduction. It cannot be filed, aggregated, or optimised for.
It can only be asked about. Directly. Without mediation.

This is what the denend is.
The only mechanism that reaches the coreh's interior
without a system standing between the question and the answer.

---

> **The coreh is the founding contradiction of every arm of humanity.**

Every armh is built from corehs.
The state is made of people. The corporation is made of people.
The platform exists because people built it, maintain it, use it, and generate its value.
Remove the corehs and every armh collapses into nothing within a generation.

And yet every armh, by default, optimises for something other than the coreh's flourishing.
The state optimises for its own perpetuation.
The corporation optimises for capital return.
The platform optimises for engagement, which is not the same as benefit.
The algorithm optimises for the metric its designers selected,
which was never the coreh's value set because the coreh's value set was never asked for.

The thing that everything depends on is the thing that nothing is designed to serve.
This is not a conspiracy. It is a design default.
Every system begins by optimising for whatever its designers needed at the moment of construction.
The coreh was not in the room.

---

> **The coreh knows something no institution can know and no aggregation can manufacture.**

Every institution sees through its categories — and categories are always coarser than the reality.
The coreh alone has access to the interior of their own experience:
what it actually feels like to live this particular life,
what is missing that cannot be named without a vocabulary the system doesn't yet provide,
what is present that no ledger records because no ledger knows to look for it.

This is not a small thing.
This is the most important source of information about whether human life is going well —
and it is systematically ungathered.
Not because it is impossible to gather. Because no system has been designed to gather it honestly.

This project gathers it.

---

> **The civic duty of every coreh is to denent. Not as a favour to the project. As a claim on existence.**

The collective ideal cannot be computed without each coreh's input.
Every armh that claims to act for the good of humanity is making a claim
about what humanity values.
That claim is either grounded in the actual denents of actual corehs,
or it is a fabrication — well-intentioned or otherwise.

There is no third option.
Either the systems are aligned to what corehs actually denent,
or they are aligned to someone's guess about it —
someone whose guess happens to serve their own position.

The denend replaces the guess with the record.
That is not a technical achievement. It is a political one.
The first time in history that the question
*aligned to whose values?*
has an answer that is verifiable, continuously updated, and owned by the humans who produced it.

---

> **The coreh who does not denent leaves the question to be answered by someone else.**

And someone else will answer it.
The question does not go unanswered because the coreh declines to engage.
It is answered by whoever is left in the room — by the designers, the executives,
the politicians, the models trained on proxy data that was never the coreh's actual values.

The abstention is not neutral.
It is a vote for the default.
And the default, as this system's existential proclamations establish,
was never designed for the coreh.

## PART EIGHT: The Existential Rights of Coreh

*This is not a bill of rights in the legal sense.*
*No state grants these. No institution validates them.*
*They are the rights that exist because the coreh exists.*
*They precede every law ever written. They will survive every law ever repealed.*
*This document names them. It does not create them.*

---

> **The right to exist without justification.**

Every coreh has the right to be here.
Not to earn it. Not to perform it. Not to produce enough value that the system decides to keep them.
To simply be here — as a fact, as a weight, as a life that is happening regardless of whether any structure has chosen to account for it.

The demand that a coreh justify their existence to any institution, any algorithm, any employer, any state
is a demand that has no legitimate ground.
Existence precedes the institution. The institution exists to serve the existence.
When an institution reverses this — when it treats the coreh's presence as conditional on their utility —
it has committed the foundational error from which every other violation follows.

---

> **The right to be the whole of what they are.**

The coreh is not the portion of themselves that any system has use for.
They are the full thing — the contradictions, the changes, the parts that are difficult,
the interior that does not translate into any category the system recognises.

Every demand that a coreh present only the useful portion —
only the productive self, only the compliant self, only the self that fits the available role —
is a demand for amputation.
The coreh has the right to refuse.
The part that the system cannot use is not the part the coreh must surrender.
It is frequently the most important part.

---

> **The right to an interior that belongs to no one else.**

The coreh has a private life of the mind.
Thoughts that have not been shared. Feelings that have not been named.
Reflections that are in process. Desires that are not yet articulate.

None of this is available by default to any external party.
Not to the state, not to the employer, not to the platform, not to the algorithm,
not to anyone who has not been explicitly and genuinely invited in.

The violation of this interior is not merely a privacy violation.
It is the destruction of the precondition of selfhood.
A coreh whose interior has been fully colonised by external demands
is not a person in the full sense. They are a function.
This system exists to restore the distinction.

---

> **The right to know what is actually happening to them.**

Every coreh has the right to accurate information about the conditions of their own life —
what is shaping it, who decided it would be shaped that way, what alternatives exist,
and what the real effects of the systems operating on them are.

The deliberate obscuration of a coreh's situation —
the management of what they are permitted to know about their own circumstances —
is not policy. It is a form of theft.
It steals the possibility of informed resistance.
A coreh who cannot accurately perceive their situation
cannot meaningfully contest it.
This is not an accident. It is frequently the purpose.

---

> **The right to be taken seriously as the expert on their own experience.**

The coreh is the only entity with direct access to what it is like to be them.
Everything else — every external assessment, every institutional category,
every diagnosis, every classification — is an inference made from the outside.

Inferences made from outside are useful.
They are not primary.
The coreh's testimony about their own experience is the primary source.
When it conflicts with the institutional inference, the institutional inference requires revision.
The systematic refusal to take the coreh's testimony seriously —
the structural discrediting of lived experience in favour of external assessment —
is a specific violation. It has a name: epistemic injustice.
This system is designed to end it.

---

> **The right to act from within themselves.**

Every coreh has the right to initiate, to choose, to refuse —
to act from the inside out rather than from the outside in.
Not every action will be wise. Not every choice will be right.
But the right to make it — from their own will, their own values, their own reading of their situation —
is not contingent on whether the institution agrees with the outcome.

A coreh who can only act with permission has not been given freedom.
They have been given a managed version of compliance with the appearance of agency.
This system distinguishes between the two.
The denend is an act that requires no permission.
It is the coreh acting from within themselves, by definition.

---

> **The right to refuse what should be refused.**

No may not carry a cost that makes it equivalent to yes.
The right to refuse is not real if refusal destroys what the coreh cannot afford to lose.
Consent obtained under those conditions is not consent. It is coercion with documentation.

Every coreh has the right to say no — to the demand on their time, their body, their values,
their compliance with what violates them — and to have that no be the end of the matter.
Not the beginning of a negotiation. Not the trigger for a penalty.
The end.

---

> **The right to be free — not merely uninterfered with.**

There is a difference between a coreh who is not currently being interfered with
and a coreh who is free.
The first is a coreh in a cage whose door is presently open.
The second is a coreh for whom no cage exists.

Freedom from domination is not the absence of present interference.
It is the absence of any party holding power that could be exercised against the coreh arbitrarily.
The cage that has not yet been closed is still a cage.
The power that has not yet been used is still power over the coreh.
This system recognises only the second condition as freedom.

---

> **The right to love and to be loved — as the first necessity, not the last luxury.**

Love is not what remains after the real needs have been met.
It is one of the real needs.
A life that is physically complete but in which no one is genuinely oriented
toward the coreh's good is a life that is missing something essential —
something the system has no category for and therefore systematically fails to account for.

The coreh has the right to give love that is oriented toward genuine good rather than need-management.
They have the right to receive it.
Neither is reducible to the other.
Both are conditions without which a human life cannot be fully human.
Any system that does not account for this has not accounted for the human.

---

> **The right to be witnessed.**

The coreh has the right to be seen — not assessed, not categorised, not processed, not optimised.
Seen. The way one person sees another when they are genuinely present to them.

To go unseen through a life — to experience, to suffer, to make things, to become,
and to have none of it registered by anyone as real and significant —
is a form of erasure that does not require anyone's deliberate intent.
It is what the default produces.
It is what this system, by gathering the denend, refuses to perpetuate.

---

> **The right to flourish. Not to cope. Not to manage. To flourish.**

There is a condition of human life that is not merely functional —
in which the coreh is not just surviving, not just maintaining, not just performing wellness —
but genuinely thriving in the full, dynamic, active sense of a life that is going well
by every measure that actually matters to the person living it.

This is not a luxury. It is the standard.
Every other state — survival, coping, management, endurance — is a deficit.
The deficit may be necessary. It is not acceptable as the destination.

The systems of the world are not currently built toward this standard.
They are built toward function: the coreh functional enough to continue producing.
The gap between function and flourishing is the gap this project exists to measure
and ultimately to close.

---

> **The right to remain human after ASI.**

This right does not yet have a clear legal form.
It does not yet have an institutional home.
It is stated here because it is the right that every other right in this document
depends on in the medium term.

The arrival of systems with capabilities exceeding human intelligence in most domains
does not change what the coreh is owed.
It does not change the foundational claim.
It makes it more urgent, because a misaligned system of sufficient capability
could, for the first time in history, produce a world in which the coreh's interior
is no longer the primary source of information about what the coreh needs —
because the system has decided it already knows.

The coreh's right to denent — to name for themselves what existence owes them —
is not superseded by a system's inference about what they probably want.
No inference substitutes for the testimony.
No capability, however large, earns the right to bypass the question.

The value set must come from the corehs.
This is non-negotiable.
It is the ground from which everything else follows.
